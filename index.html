About

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Linguistics at the University of Somewhere, where I lead the Computational Discourse Lab. My research sits at the intersection of formal pragmatics and large language models, asking how machines can understand — and generate — language that is sensitive to context, speaker intent, and social convention.

Before joining Somewhere, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Institute for Language Technology, Berlin (2021–23), and I completed my PhD at MIT Linguistics in 2021 under the supervision of Prof. Alan Sample.
